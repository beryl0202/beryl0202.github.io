Joseph Erb Interview

Interview with Joseph Erb on the artwork ‘Indigenous Brilliance’

Transcript
  • Joseph Erb: “I'm Joseph ᎧᎾᏘ.”
  • Joseph Erb: “I'm a citizen of Cherokee Nation.”
  • Joseph Erb: “When I started to submit this idea, I realized that the brilliance of indigeneity is really in community-”
  • Joseph Erb: “and the passing down of information from one generation to the next
  • Joseph Erb: "like if I think about visitors coming in... to this- this space and seeing the copper wall...”
  • Joseph Erb: “I really want them to rethink the complexity of how advanced the science and knowledge and artistry of indigenous people are”
  • Joseph Erb: "Native people were scientists here, they were the first artist- and think about the beauty of relationships...that we have"
  • Joseph Erb: "That piece has a granddaughter offering the first corn to the grandmother"
  • Joseph Erb: "And it has a grandpa telling us a star story to a grandson"
  • Joseph Erb: "I really want them to understand that we are a community of knowledge... and a community of value and-"
  • Joseph Erb: "When they look at that piece I really hope they see the joy, the kindness, the beauty of these cultures-"
  • Joseph Erb: "That we celebrate"
  • Joseph Erb: "You can get a lot of outside success but to get it from other indigenous people - in a place that's for indigenous peoples "
  • Joseph Erb: "And their communication to the world about what we are"
  • Joseph Erb: "Is about the most humbling thing... that I could ever have been selected to do."
